# Xiaohei Editorial Illustration Style Guide

Source authority studied: `helloianneo/ian-xiaohei-illustrations` on GitHub, especially its skill, style DNA, Xiaohei IP notes, composition patterns, and QA checklist.

## Visual language

- Use a pure white page. No cream tint, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, glass, or app-dashboard surfaces.
- Build the image like an editorial article sketch: one clear cognitive idea per panel, lots of quiet whitespace, and a slightly absurd physical metaphor that makes the idea readable in about one second.
- Primary marks are black hand-drawn lines: thin, uneven, slightly wobbly, and naive. Avoid perfect geometric vector polish.
- Keep color scarce. Red is for warnings/results/emphasis, orange is for paths/arrows/flow, and blue is for secondary notes or system-like explanation.
- Handwritten annotations should be short and sparse. Prefer a few words near the object over paragraph explanations.

## Xiaohei character

- Xiaohei is a solid black creature with an irregular blob/bean body, tiny white dot eyes, thin stick legs, and occasional thin arms.
- The expression is blank, serious, deadpan, and a little awkward; Xiaohei is not cute, glossy, emoji-like, or a mascot.
- Xiaohei must participate in the scene's core action: pointing, carrying, burying, tugging, weighing, building, reacting, labeling, or operating the metaphor.
- If the panel still fully works after removing Xiaohei, redesign it so Xiaohei is doing the meaning-bearing action.

## Composition rules for this Remotion demo

- Treat each timed scene as a moving editorial panel on a white page.
- Keep the subject around the middle of the vertical frame with generous margins; avoid filling the whole canvas.
- Use only a few objects per panel: bags, arrows, simple figures, a dirt mound, a moral box, or a simple story map.
- Prefer irregular hand-drawn SVG paths, rough rectangles, wobbly arrows, and slightly offset duplicate strokes over polished icons.
- Transitions should feel like sliding across a sketchbook page, not a tech deck.

## What to avoid

- Polished vector mascot art, emoji style, corporate UI, dashboard graphics, startup gradients, biblical costumes, and generic Sunday-school cartoon scenes.
- Dense flowcharts, PPT infographic layouts, too many nodes/arrows, formal titles inside the drawing, and explanatory paragraphs.
- Overbuilding historical details; the parable should read through simple article-style metaphors, not costume design.
